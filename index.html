Profile

SHEET 01 / 08

Dr. R. Pandiyarajan is a mechanical engineering academic based in Chennai, currently serving as Dean, Research & Development at Agni College of Technology. Over thirteen years across four institutions, his work has stayed close to one question: how do you join and characterise hybrid metal matrix composites so they hold up under real load?

His doctoral research, completed part-time at Anna University while teaching full time, examined friction stir welding of AA6061 reinforced with ZrO₂ and carbon particulate. That same experimental discipline — stir-casting, welding parameter optimisation, microstructure and fatigue testing — now runs through funded projects in agricultural drones, vehicle-reliability electronics, and coral-reef monitoring ROVs.

He is a recognised Anna University Ph.D. supervisor, a peer reviewer for three international journals, and a guest editor for the American Journal of Mechanical Engineering.

Friction Stir Welding

Process parameter optimisation, dissimilar and hybrid aluminium & magnesium joints, FSSW fixtures.

Metal Matrix Composites

ZrO₂, B4C, SiC and graphite reinforced AA6061/AA6082 — stir casting, mechanical & tribological behaviour.

Additive Manufacturing

3D-printing filament from biodegradable fibre composites; custom hearing-aid component production.

Thermal & Fatigue Analysis

PCM ceiling thermal management, microchannel heat sinks, fatigue crack growth in alloy steels.
